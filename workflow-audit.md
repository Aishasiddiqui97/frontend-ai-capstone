# Reflection

Auditing my workflow revealed a clear pattern: I spend a disproportionate amount of time on tasks that AI can handle efficiently — reading documentation, drafting commits, and researching libraries — while undervaluing the human aspects of planning and self-assessment. AI saves the most time in two areas: accelerating comprehension of unfamiliar code or documentation, and generating boilerplate or repetitive content that would otherwise consume hours. However, human judgement remains essential for architectural decisions, prioritising daily work, and honestly evaluating my own learning progress. No AI can decide what I should focus on next or whether I truly understand a concept. Moving forward, I plan to formalise the delegation patterns identified in this audit — using Claude for structured drafting and ChatGPT for research — while protecting time for reflection and deep work. I will revisit this document each sprint to recalibrate classifications and track progress against the three target tasks, ensuring my workflow evolves alongside my skills.
